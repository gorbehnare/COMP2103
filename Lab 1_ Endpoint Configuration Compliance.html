Lab 1: Endpoint Configuration Compliance

OS Hardening using CIS Benchmarks

If you are designing a system, you can ensure security by having a full understanding of all underlying technology in your environment and protocols. Acquiring all the required knowledge is certainly to strife for, but in reality, due to the vast amount of available information and knowledge, the best most of us can achieve is to have general knowledge in some areas and specialize in a narrow field. Several organizations (such as CIS and OpenSCAP) are specialized in consulting with professionals and members with expertise in various fields and using consensus processes to create security configuration "benchmarks". These recommendations are available to individuals and organizations to assist in designing and configuring secure systems.

For this lab we will be looking at the CIS benchmarks, and later on evaluate our system using SCA profiles.

Conclusions and Final Thoughts

  • Looking at the list of failed and passed items in Wazuh, can you find out if having a 100% compliant system possible with the default settings and adding any exceptions?
    • This should clearly demonstrate that security is porous, and complete security does not exist.
    • This also demonstrates that sometimes security comes at the cost of functionality and convenience.
      • Revisit section 4.1.7 Ensure ufw default deny firewall policy (Automated) where the default deny rule for outbound traffic will break functionality.
  • Looking at the failed items, you may find that some of them may not seem very "risky" in many situations.
    • Although there is a rationale behind security configuration recommendations, they are generic in nature and do not take specific situations and needs into consideration.
    • Risk is variable, and is very specific to individual systems and situations.
  • If you are asked to create systems with complete and rigorous compliance requirements that not all systems can meet, a risk assessment report on non-complying systems and failed items are probably the best way to communicate the reasons behind non-compliance to the authorizing authorities and management. This may allow you to get approval for "exceptions", although at cost of some additional paperwork!
